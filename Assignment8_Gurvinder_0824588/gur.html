Shimla

Shimla is the capital and the largest city of the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. Shimla was declared as the summer capital of British India in 1864. Shimla is located at an altitude of 2276 m above the sea level on seven different hills. Shimla is one of the youngest cities in India and the principal commercial, cultural and educational centre of the state.

  • Chail
  • Theog
  • Chadwick Falls
  • Naldehra and Shaily Peak.

Things to do in Shimla

  • The Ridge and Mall Road:
  • Take a leisurely stroll along The Ridge and Mall Road. These are the main shopping streets in Shimla, offering a range of shops, cafes, and entertainment.
  • Jakhu Temple:
  • Visit Jakhu Temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman. It is situated on Jakhu Hill and offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and the town.
  • Christ Church:
  • Explore Christ Church on The Ridge, one of the oldest churches in North India. The neo-Gothic architecture and beautiful stained glass windows make it a must-visit.

What is Shimla known for

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh in India, is renowned for its captivating blend of colonial charm, picturesque landscapes, and pleasant climate. The city, once the summer capital of British India, boasts a remarkable collection of colonial-era architecture, including the Viceregal Lodge and Christ Church, reflecting its historical significance.

History

Shimla's history unfolds as a captivating narrative that spans centuries and reflects diverse influences. Originally named Shyamala after the Hindu goddess, the area was transformed during the British colonial era when it emerged as a cool retreat from the plains' heat. Recognizing its potential, the British established Shimla as the summer capital in 1864, leading to the construction of colonial-style structures, including the iconic Viceregal Lodge and Christ Church. The Kalka-Shimla Railway, built in the late 19th century, added to the city's charm and accessibility, earning it recognition as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Post-independence, Shimla became the capital of Himachal Pradesh in 1966. Beyond its political significance, Shimla has evolved into a popular tourist destination, preserving its colonial architecture, such as the Ridge and Gaiety Theatre, and embracing cultural institutions like the Indian Institute of Advanced Study.
